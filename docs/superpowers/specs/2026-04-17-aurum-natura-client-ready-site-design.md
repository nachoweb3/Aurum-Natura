# Aurum Natura Client-Ready Website Redesign

Date: 2026-04-17
Status: Approved in conversation, written spec pending user review

## Goal

Transform Aurum Natura from a technically functional premium prototype into a fully client-facing commercial website written for end customers. The site must feel complete, coherent, and ready to sell. It must present Aurum Natura as a premium finca-origin food brand for households that want to eat better every week, while still carrying enough refinement and visual quality to appeal to gourmet buyers.

The website will prioritize subscription sales, while preserving one-time purchase as a secondary route. The copy, layout, imagery, and page structure must stop feeling like internal scaffolding and start feeling like a finished premium storefront.

## Core Positioning

Aurum Natura is presented as a curated finca subscription from Nombela, Toledo, built around:

- huevos camperos de gallinas de raza
- verdura de temporada
- hortaliza de temporada
- fruta de temporada
- origen real y producción limitada

The emotional promise is not luxury for its own sake. The real commercial promise is this: a better way for households to receive carefully selected seasonal food with visible origin and a stronger sense of trust, quality, and rhythm.

## Commercial Direction

### Primary conversion model

The website sells `suscripción` first and `compra puntual` second.

This means:

- the homepage leads with weekly or recurring household value
- product pages and pricing tables present subscription as the best-value and most natural option
- one-time purchase remains available as a low-friction first purchase path
- calls to action steer users toward joining, reserving, or starting a subscription rather than simply "asking for information"

### Audience priority

The site serves two audiences:

1. families and couples who want to eat better every week
2. premium/gourmet customers who buy based on origin, selection, and quality

The writing and UX must emphasize the first audience. The second audience is captured through visual refinement, ingredient language, price positioning, and trust-building detail.

## Tone of Voice

The copy must be written for customers, never for the project owner or for technical review.

### Required tone

- clear
- elegant
- warm
- direct
- commercially mature
- confident without sounding theatrical

### Avoid

- placeholder language
- implementation language
- poetic exaggeration that weakens trust
- internal explanations
- abstract luxury clichés with no product substance

### Writing principle

Every section must answer one of these customer questions:

- Why should I trust this?
- Why is it worth the price?
- What will I receive?
- How does it fit into my week?
- Why is this better than buying elsewhere?

## Visual Direction

The visual system should feel like `finca cercana con nivel premium`.

It must combine:

- rural authenticity
- editorial polish
- calm, high-value composition
- practical clarity near conversion points

### Visual characteristics

- cinematic but believable imagery
- natural texture, warm light, real produce, real surfaces
- no generic "healthy startup" look
- no rustic craft-market amateurism
- no obvious placeholder composition

### Temporary media strategy

Until real photography is available, the site will use AI-generated imagery with a consistent art direction:

- finca landscapes and orchard scenes
- egg gathering and produce preparation scenes
- premium produce still lifes
- carefully styled subscription box imagery
- household kitchen and table scenes for families and couples

The AI imagery must be cohesive enough that the site feels intentional, not patched together from mixed styles.

## Information Architecture

The first release keeps four public pages:

- `index.html`
- `productos.html`
- `sobre-nosotros.html`
- `contacto.html`

These pages must be rewritten and redesigned to feel complete rather than expanded with disconnected components.

## Homepage

### Job of the page

The homepage must act as the main selling surface for first-time visitors. Its purpose is to establish trust fast, explain the offer clearly, justify premium pricing, and route users into subscription or trial purchase.

### Required structure

#### 1. Hero

The hero must immediately communicate:

- Aurum Natura is a finca-origin food subscription
- it is based in Nombela, Toledo
- it is premium, seasonal, and limited
- it is for people who want to eat better at home

The hero copy should be practical and desirable, not mystical.

#### 2. Value proposition

A compact block that explains the offer in plain terms:

- curated seasonal produce
- free-range eggs from heritage-breed hens
- premium selection for home cooking
- recurring delivery or one-time box

#### 3. Why families choose it

A benefit section focused on the household audience:

- better weekly eating habits
- less supermarket dependence
- seasonal variety without extra decision fatigue
- confidence in what enters the home

#### 4. Subscription-first offer block

This section introduces the buying model:

- weekly subscription
- biweekly subscription
- one-time purchase

The hierarchy must visually and verbally favor subscription.

#### 5. Featured boxes

This block presents the main offers with pricing and clear comparison.

#### 6. Origin and trust block

A section that grounds the brand in:

- Calle Viana 54
- Nombela
- Toledo
- Castilla-La Mancha

The copy should frame the address as part of authenticity and traceability, not as a dry contact detail.

#### 7. Seasonal rhythm / how it works

A short process section:

- choose a box
- receive seasonal selection
- cook and enjoy during the week
- continue through subscription or reorder

#### 8. Social proof / reassurance

Prepared for testimonial cards and credibility language. It should feel like customer reassurance, not fake hype.

#### 9. Final conversion block

A closing CTA with clean, premium urgency:

- subscribe now
- reserve this week’s box
- try your first box

## Products Page

### Job of the page

This page must function as the commercial comparison surface. It should help customers choose the right box and understand why subscription is the better option.

### Product strategy

The first release should center on three premium box tiers:

- `Caja Esencial` — entry premium tier for couples or small households
- `Caja Familiar` — primary subscription tier for family weekly use
- `Caja Reserva` — high-end curated tier for households that want the fullest experience

### Pricing direction

Prices should read as deliberately premium, not mass-market. The first release will position them approximately at:

- Caja Esencial: 49 EUR
- Caja Familiar: 79 EUR
- Caja Reserva: 119 EUR

One-time purchase can be presented at a slightly less favorable price perception than subscription through messaging, badge hierarchy, or plan framing. The site does not need to show discount math unless the final sales model requires it.

### Required content

Each offer card should include:

- name
- price
- who it is for
- typical contents
- subscription emphasis
- one-time purchase availability
- premium value cues
- CTA

### Product gallery

The image treatment must move from "component demo" to "catalog presentation". Images should help sell the produce and the box experience, not merely prove that a lightbox exists.

### Supporting sections

The page should also include:

- what usually comes in the boxes
- why the contents change with the season
- why premium pricing is justified
- common objections and answers

## About Page

### Job of the page

The page must convert skepticism into trust. It should present Aurum Natura as a real place, run by real people, with a real philosophy of production.

### Required themes

- origin in Nombela, Toledo
- respect for seasonality
- smaller-scale production
- egg quality and breed-based differentiation
- selected fruit, vegetables, and garden produce
- slower, more careful handling

### Tone

It should feel grounded, not romanticized. The page should not read like a manifesto detached from the product. It should answer:

- where does this come from?
- why is it different?
- why does that difference matter?

### Visual expectations

This page is the best place for:

- finca imagery
- land and orchard scenes
- produce handling
- preparation moments
- testimonial rhythm

## Contact Page

### Job of the page

The contact page must feel like customer support for a premium direct-to-consumer brand, not a generic placeholder form.

### Required elements

- WhatsApp as the main fast-contact route
- email as secondary channel
- location and origin details
- concise FAQs
- delivery and consultation language

### Delivery language

Because exact zone coverage is not fully confirmed, the copy should avoid overpromising. The wording should remain commercial but safe:

- scheduled deliveries
- availability depending on area
- subscription and first-box enquiries handled directly

This keeps the site sellable without inventing unsupported logistics promises.

## Copy Rules by Topic

### Pricing

Pricing must sound intentional and premium. The copy should explain value through:

- seasonal curation
- production limits
- origin trust
- household convenience
- ingredient quality

It must not sound apologetic about price.

### Subscription

Subscription should be framed as the best way to maintain a better table at home week after week. It should be described as:

- practical
- curated
- recurring
- premium

Do not introduce permanence claims, cancellation claims, or discount promises unless those operations are confirmed.

### Seasonal contents

The site should state clearly that the exact selection varies. The copy should make this sound desirable and natural, not vague.

### Eggs

Egg language should be strong and specific:

- huevos camperos
- gallinas de raza
- quality and origin emphasis

Avoid technical claims that would require legal or certification proof if not confirmed.

## Conversion Strategy

### Primary CTA language

Preferred CTA verbs:

- Suscribirme
- Reservar mi caja
- Empezar con Aurum Natura
- Probar mi primera caja

### Secondary CTA language

- Comprar una sola vez
- Consultar disponibilidad
- Hablar por WhatsApp

The visual hierarchy must always place subscription first.

## UX Principles

### Must feel finished

The site must no longer read like a collection of implemented components. Every section should feel authored, paced, and intentionally sequenced.

### Must stay clear

Even with strong visuals and premium tone, pages must remain easy to scan. Families should understand the offer without effort.

### Must stay credible

No fake scarcity language, no obvious overpromising, and no text that sounds like internal hype. The trust of the first-time buyer matters more than stylistic flourish.

## Implementation Constraints

The redesign should preserve the static site architecture but upgrade:

- page composition
- copy
- section hierarchy
- media quality
- conversion logic

The project should remain easy to deploy as a static site.

## First Release Outcome

The finished first release must make Aurum Natura look like a real premium subscription brand that is already selling, not preparing to sell.

When a customer lands on the site, they should understand:

- what Aurum Natura is
- why the product is premium
- who it is for
- why subscription is the best route
- how to take the next step immediately

## Explicit Assumptions for This Release

To avoid blocking implementation, the following assumptions are accepted for the first release:

- subscription is the main commercial route
- one-time purchase remains available
- pricing is intentionally premium
- contents are seasonal and variable
- WhatsApp remains the primary closing channel
- AI imagery is acceptable until real finca photography is ready
- delivery wording will stay commercially useful but operationally cautious

These assumptions should guide implementation unless the user overrides them before the build begins.
